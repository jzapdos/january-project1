Event 2: Aug 12, 30BC, Cleopatra Committed Suicide

On August 12 B.C., Cleopatra, Egypt’s last queen, died by suicide after her defeat by Octavian, Rome’s future first emperor. Her lover, Mark Antony, fatally wounded himself believing she was already dead, and he died in her arms. Cleopatra, refusing to be paraded as a captive, likely ended her life with the bite of a venomous asp, a symbol of royalty. Her death marked the fall of the Ptolemaic dynasty and Egypt’s annexation by Rome. Octavian executed her son Caesarion and used her wealth to secure his power, later becoming Augustus and ushering in a new Roman era.
