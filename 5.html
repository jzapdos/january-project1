Event 4: Aug 12, 1953, the Soviet Union tested their first thermonuclear bomb.

The Soviet Union developed hydrogen bombs starting with the "Layer Cake" design, involving layers of hydrogen fuel and uranium, which limited explosive power. Their first fusion-based bomb, tested on August 12, 1953, had a 400-kiloton yield and was compact enough for aerial deployment. Later, inspired by radiation implosion, they shifted to more advanced designs. On November 22, 1955, the Soviets detonated their first true hydrogen bomb with a 1.6-megaton yield. This progression culminated on October 23, 1961, with a massive 58-megaton explosion, showcasing the peak of Soviet thermonuclear capabilities.
