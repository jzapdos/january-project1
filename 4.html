Event 3: Aug 12, 1908, The first Model T Ford is built

Henry Ford's Model T revolutionized transportation by making automobiles affordable for the average worker. Introduced in 1908 at Ford’s Piquette Avenue Plant, the Model T was practical, efficient, and inexpensive, with early models priced at $825. Production scaled rapidly, and by 1925, a Model T cost just $260. The car’s success stemmed from innovations like the moving assembly line, which boosted manufacturing efficiency. Over 15 million Model Ts were built before production ended in 1927, marking the car as a milestone in automotive history. It earned the title "Car of the Century" in 1999 for its profound societal impact.
